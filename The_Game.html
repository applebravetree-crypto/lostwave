The Game was a french band active in the late 1980s, led by singer Marc Dimitrievitch & guitarist Patrick Larrieu. They released their only album, Under The White Bible Law, in 1988, under the Comotion Musique label. The Game broke up in 1990.[2]

I discovered this band, along with their song "Walk Away", around April 2026, from a Tor's Cabinet of Curiosities video outro. This led me to a YouTube channel named TheGameFanPage. They have 4 videos:

  1. The official music-video for "Walk Away" – uploaded on 31 May 2011;
  2. The Game performing "Under The White Bible Law" on a TV-show called Télé Caroline – uploaded on 22 September 2012;
  3. The Game performing "Walk Away" at the Marché International du Disque et de l'Édition Musicale (MIDEM) in Cannes, France, on 20 January 1989 – uploaded on 31 January 2018;
  4. The Game performing "Walk Away" at something called Midi PIC in France in 1988 – uploaded on 10 December 2023;

At the end of Video 2, the host of Télé Caroline says The Game, and the first album, which indicates that, if not their only album, Under The White Bible Law was their first.

Video 4 suggests that "Walk Away", & therefore also their album Under The White Bible Law, were released before 1989.

June–July 2026: Under The White Bible Law & The Game's break-up

On 28 June 2026, while searching for videos of "Walk Away" on YouTube, I found a video of Under The White Bible Law, the only known-so-far album by The Game. It was uploaded by a channel named Under The Waves, a "channel that is dedicated to uploading obscure 80s music that is either underrated, hard to find or unavailable on streaming services or YouTube". This was the first evidence of songs by The Game other than "Walk Away" & "Under The White Bible Law".

1 July 2026 was when I really started digging for information. Hence, 1 July has several discoveries, all listed below.

I found a page on Discogs which lists the songs in this album, strongly confirming that it did in-fact exist. The aforementioned video on YouTube also lists the songs in its description—though without "Over The Sea"— but this page once again strongly confirms their veracity.

I found a page on a website called Romu Rocks!, which gives us more information on The Game. Most significantly, it reveals that The Game broke up in 1990, after 1 album. We now know that Under The White Bible Law is The Game's only album.

I found what I assume is probably the official music-video of "Under The White Bible Law" from a YouTube channel called Mes Volum. It also includes the lyrics in its description.
